#### THE PROBLEM

# Your energy is trapped at your meter

4.3 million Australian households generate their own electricity. But every kilowatt-hour they produce is **locked to one address**.

Their solar earns value — but only at home. Their battery arbitrages — but only at home. Step off the property and that value disappears.

**Every platform in the market optimises at the meter. Nobody has broken energy free from the address.**

#### THE MODEL

# The grid pays. You earn. citEther takes a margin on value created.

<div class="columns">

**How it works**

When energy travels from your roof to your mum's flat, there are transmission and distribution charges — real third-party network fees. citEther accounts for these. The consumer sees net value after costs.

Even after network fees, Follow Me Power **still pays better than a zero-cent feed-in tariff** — because the alternative is exporting for nothing.

**Revenue:**
— **Consumer margin** — citEther takes a share of the delta between buy-low and sell-high
— **DNSP capacity payments** — each kW of managed load is a transformer upgrade deferred
— **Grid services** — frequency response, demand management, coordinated curtailment

**Why the grid pays**

Coordinated DER is cheaper than building gas peakers ($2B+ per facility). 100,000 managed batteries provide more flexible capacity than a single peaking plant, at a fraction of the cost, deployable in minutes.

When energy stays within a Pod — same transformer, same feeder — network charges shrink because the electrons never leave the local loop.

</div>
