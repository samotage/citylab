# citEther — Base44 Website Prompt (Variant 3: Apple Energy)

## Design Direction: Immersive Product Story

Inspired by Apple product pages (iPhone, Vision Pro). Full-bleed sections, cinematic scroll experience, product-centric storytelling. Each section is a full viewport that tells one idea with one visual. The scroll IS the narrative. Bold photography-style 3D renders mixed with crisp data overlays.

**Visual signature:** Alternating dark/light full-viewport sections. Large-scale 3D-rendered illustrations of homes, EVs, and batteries as hero visuals. Text appears as you scroll — reveal animations tied to scroll position. Sticky elements that transform as you scroll past them. Confident, aspirational, premium consumer brand.

---

## Brand

**Name:** citEther (lowercase c-i-t, capital E)
**Logo:** Hashtag/grid geometric mark — interconnection symbol
**Tagline:** "Tether to the grid. Get paid."
**Fonts:** Inter for text, JetBrains Mono for numbers/data
**Colours:** Violet #7C3AED primary, Teal #0D9488 secondary, Dark #09090B, White #FFFFFF. Use colour sparingly — let the illustrations carry the visual weight.

## Animation Summary

Scroll-driven is the primary animation model. No idle animations except the Follow Me Power map.

| Element | Animation | Trigger |
|---------|-----------|---------|
| Hero image | Parallax (reveals street) | Scroll |
| Stats | Fade-in/scale, one at a time | Scroll-locked (33% each) |
| Problem money icons | Drift upward continuously | On section visible |
| Step sections | Fade-in from bottom | Scroll |
| Follow Me Power EV | Drives route, prices appear | Scroll into view, loops |
| Community connections | Violet lines draw between homes | Scroll |
| Sankey flow | Lines animate left to right | Scroll |
| CTA street | Homes light up sequentially | Scroll |

## Key Differentiator from Other Variants

This variant tells the story through ILLUSTRATION, not through cards and text layouts. Every section has a hero visual that carries 70% of the communication. Text is secondary — it confirms what the image already told you. This is the most expensive to build but the most visually memorable.
