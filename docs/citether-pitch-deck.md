# citEther — Pitch Deck (3 Minutes, P-A-S)

## Pitch Overview

**Product:** citEther — community energy platform connecting consumer energy resources directly to grid value
**Audience:** Energy market experts who understand the problem space
**Format:** 3 minutes, Problem-Agitate-Solution
**Primary customer:** Consumers (solar/battery/EV households)
**Secondary customer:** DNSPs (avoided network infrastructure)
**Revenue model:** Margin on energy arbitrage — Uber model for electrons
**Headline innovation:** Follow Me Power — location-based V2G earnings
**Core engine:** Auto-Arb — set-and-forget arbitrage with consumer comfort guardrails (from SEG v2.0)

---

## Beat Sheet

### BEAT 1: PROBLEM (20 seconds)

**Opening hook (10s):**
"Four million households invested their own capital in solar and batteries. They're now the largest generation fleet in the NEM — and they have zero market power. Their batteries get dispatched without consent, they export into negative prices, and the value their flexibility creates flows to everyone except them."

**The death spiral (10s):**
"You know the utility death spiral. It's not theoretical anymore. Every household that disconnects pushes costs onto those who can't. And every bad experience with a VPP or a retailer's opaque portal accelerates that decision."

**Key data (weave in, don't list):**
- 4.3M solar households, 450K with batteries
- 7 TWh curtailed in 2025
- 31% of NEM intervals now negative price
- Average VPP credit: $200–$1,000/yr — most of the value extracted upstream

---

### BEAT 2: AGITATE (20 seconds)

**The coordination failure (10s):**
"The grid needs coordinated distributed energy. But every manufacturer runs a proprietary stack. Every retailer has a walled garden. The consumer faces a wall of incompatible systems with zero transparency. You can't coordinate what you can't see, and you can't see behind the meter."

**The trust deficit (10s):**
"This isn't a technology problem — it's a trust problem. Consumers who've been burned don't participate. And without consumer participation, the transition stalls. Seven terawatt-hours of clean energy curtailed last year because the coordination layer doesn't exist."

---

### BEAT 3: SOLUTION (80 seconds)

**The product (15s):**
"citEther steps outside the proprietary loop. Direct-to-endpoint connection — we talk to inverters, batteries, EVs, smart HVAC, hot water systems, pool pumps — every controllable asset in the home. For commercial and industrial customers, that extends to warehouse cooling loads, compressors, and flexible process plant. One platform that orchestrates every smart asset on the network and connects them to real grid value."

**Auto-Arb engine — how the consumer earns (20s):**
"At the heart of citEther is a set-and-forget arbitrage engine. It doesn't just manage your battery — it orchestrates every smart asset: pre-cools your home before a price spike, shifts your hot water heating to midday solar surplus, schedules your pool pump and EV charging to the cheapest window. For an industrial customer, it chills the warehouse down and lets it coast through the peak. All automatic, all with comfort guardrails — never discharge below your floor, override when the air con is running, one-tap pause for guests. One family on Amber had their entire year's savings wiped out in a single hot day. That can't happen with citEther."

**Follow Me Power — headline innovation (15s):**
"Follow Me Power. Your energy follows you. A tradie's home solar powers their job site tools across town — through the grid, settled by citEther. An EV plugged in at Dandenong earns $50 for the hour at the local node rate. Drive to Springvale where constraint is tighter — $80. Your home energy account is no longer tied to your meter. It's tied to you, wherever you consume or supply."

*[DEMO MOMENT — show Follow Me Power map if available]*

**The triangle — one consumer, three revenue streams (5s):**
"Solar fills your home battery during the day. Across town, your car earns at a high-demand node, or your tools draw power on a job site — all settled through the grid against your home account. The grid is the wire, citEther is the settlement. Multiple locations, one account."

**Community layer + social norming (15s):**
"Coordination without community is just another dispatch system. citEther organises households into Pods — your street as its own microgrid. Can't afford a home battery? Buy a share in your Pod's community battery — use it as virtual storage to offset your peak bills, or just take the arbitrage returns as cash. Same grid events, same value, no $15,000 upfront. A renter with no roof can invest and earn. Social norming shows how you compare to your neighbours — the same mechanism that drove 2-3% sustained reduction in the Opower trials, applied to grid participation. Leaderboards, collective rewards. Everyone participates, not just those who can afford the hardware."

**Business model (15s):**
"Revenue model: citEther takes a margin on the value it creates — the delta between what the grid pays for coordinated DER and what flows through to consumers. DNSPs pay for avoided network upgrades — every kilowatt of flexible load we manage is a transformer upgrade deferred. Consumers pay nothing upfront. They earn."

---

### BEAT 4: EVIDENCE & CTA (20 seconds)

**Why this works (10s):**
"We built the intelligence layer at this hackathon. Live NEM data ingestion, real-time price signals, grid inertia proxy from synchronous generation fraction, demand response tracking. The arbitrage brain and the grid-reading infrastructure are working."

**CTA (10s):**
"What we need next: a distribution network partner to validate locational value signals against real network constraints. And a community pilot — one suburb, one hundred households, three months. Prove the coordination model works where it matters: behind the meter, with real people, earning real value."

## Speaker Notes — Delivery Guidance

**Tone:** Direct, confident, peer-to-peer. These judges know the problem — don't lecture. Talk like a founder presenting to a room of people who've tried to solve this and hit the same walls.

**Pacing:**
- Problem: fast, acknowledging. You're naming what they already know. Don't dwell.
- Agitate: the coordination failure and trust deficit. This is where you show you understand WHY previous attempts failed.
- Auto-Arb: this is the technical credibility beat. Show you understand the consumer's operational burden and have solved it.
- Follow Me Power: slow down here. This is the "oh, that's clever" moment. Give it room.
- Community: warm, human. Shift register from technical to grassroots.
- CTA: specific, concrete. One suburb, one hundred households.

**The "one hot day" anecdote:**
This is the single strongest line in the pitch for expert judges. They know exactly what this refers to — a family on Amber wholesale pricing whose air conditioning drew through a $15/kWh spike while nobody was monitoring the battery. Auto-Arb's guardrails prevent this by design. The anecdote makes the guardrail story tangible. Don't rush it.

**What expert judges are scoring:**
1. **Problem understanding** — they know the problem. They're scoring whether YOU know it. Precision > passion with this audience.
2. **Solution differentiation** — what makes this different from existing VPPs and DER aggregators? Direct-to-endpoint (no retailer middleman) + auto-arbitrage with guardrails + community layer + Follow Me Power.
3. **Feasibility** — "built at this hackathon" with live NEM data and a working arbitrage engine demonstrates technical credibility.
4. **Impact at scale** — 4.3M households is the beachhead. HVAC, industrial load-shifting, commercial sites expand the TAM.

**What to avoid:**
- Explaining concepts they already know (NEM structure, death spiral mechanics, what DER is)
- Overselling the prototype — it's a grid intelligence layer with an arbitrage engine, not a finished consumer app. Own that.
- The $2.1B curtailment number — not defensible at ~$300/MWh implied. Use 7 TWh and let them do the maths.
- Competitor bashing — don't mention specific VPPs or retailers. Just show what citEther does differently.
- Prototype apologies — never say "it's just a hackathon project." Show what you built with confidence.

## SEG v2.0 Integration Notes

The Auto-Arb Pilot from SEG v2.0 research maps directly onto citEther's consumer engine:

| SEG v2.0 Concept | citEther Integration |
|-------------------|---------------------|
| Set-and-forget arbitrage | Core dispatch engine — charges low, discharges high, automatically |
| Comfort guardrails (min SoC, HVAC override, guest mode) | Consumer trust mechanism — "your comfort is never sacrificed for arbitrage" |
| Brand-agnostic connectivity (Modbus/MQTT, multi-inverter) | Direct-to-endpoint architecture — no proprietary lock-in |
| Post-hoc savings reports | Transparency layer — "what happened, why, what you earned" |
| V2H/V2G readiness bridge | Follow Me Power — the mobility extension |
| Neighbourhood Energy Squads | Community layer — leaderboards, events, local cooperatives |

**Consumer pain points validated by SEG v2.0 research (Reddit-sourced):**
- "I already have a full time job" — babysitting arbitrage is unsustainable
- "My family erased the entire year's savings in one day" — guardrails needed
- "8–10 years of consistently being on your phone… isn't sustainable" — set-and-forget is the product
- "All of the ones you listed lock you down to the brand's components" — brand-agnostic is the architecture
- "I would always rather have a system that can be accessed locally" — local-first, no forced cloud
